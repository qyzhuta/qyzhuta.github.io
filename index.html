Recruitment Announcement

Graduate students

Our research group at the University of Texas at Dallas is seeking motivated graduate students pursuing their PhD degrees, offering Research and Teaching Assistantships to support graduate study. Ideal candidates should have a strong background in physics, having completed core physics courses in their undergraduate studies. Degrees and experience in space physics, physics, atmospheric sciences, or applied mathematics are highly encouraged. We welcome students interested in Earth's upper atmosphere, ground-based and satellite data analysis, and numerical simulation. Prospective students are encouraged to contact Dr. Qingyu Zhu directly to discuss research opportunities that align with their background and interests.


More information can be found at https://www.utdallas.edu/fact-sheets/nsm/phd-physics

Teaching

PHYS2326 Electromagnetism and Waves

Semesters: 2024Spring

Topics include electrostatics and electromagnetics, electric field and potential, electric currents, magnetic fields, laws of Coulomb, Ampere, and Faraday, Maxwell's theory of wave propagation. Two lectures per week.

PHYS1302 College Physics II

Semesters: 2024Fall

Topics include electricity, magnetism, and optics. May not be used to satisfy degree requirements for majors in the School of Engineering and Computer Science.
